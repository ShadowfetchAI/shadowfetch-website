Bird Hunter

Private birding with a real field guide backbone.

Bird Hunter turns a large species dataset into a fast field filter, a photo-backed life list, optional private iCloud sync, and a compare-ready birding notebook. It is local-first, account-free, and explicit about where the data comes from.

Identify engine

Filter by relative size, habitat, behavior, migration, and region to narrow a large catalog quickly in the field.

Capture and log

Add a sighting, attach a bird photo, keep both a fast thumbnail and a higher-quality detail image, and use current-location assist when you want the log entry filled quickly.

Life list and compare

Track photographed birds, queue trophy cards, compare species side by side, browse large data collections like Threat Watch, and optionally sync records through private iCloud storage.

Built for submission.

The current build is version 1.0 (1) with local-only storage, no accounts, no analytics, explicit BIRDBASE and AVONET attribution, and the required support and privacy pages.

Why It’s Different

A birding app that stays honest.

  • Bird Hunter does not claim AI photo identification. You filter the catalog, confirm a species, then attach your own photo to that specific bird.
  • The app keeps your sighting notes, favorites, compare board, photos, and queued share cards local to the device unless you actively share something.
  • The catalog currently contains 11,908 merged species records built from BIRDBASE and AVONET, with 10,690 direct AVONET joins over the BIRDBASE backbone.
  • The BIRDBASE and AVONET dataset names stay visible in the app and on this site because their open licenses still require attribution to accompany the information.
