Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 8, 2026

Bird Hunter is designed as a local-first birding app. It does not create accounts and does not require a developer-run cloud service to log sightings or store your private life list.

Information We Collect

Bird Hunter does not directly collect personal information from users and does not include analytics or advertising SDKs.

Information Stored On Your Device

Bird Hunter stores the following items locally on your device:

  • favorites and compare-board selections
  • sighting logs with date, count, notes, confidence, and optional location details
  • photos you attach to a species record
  • generated thumbnails and higher-quality detail images
  • queued trophy cards for later sharing

iCloud Sync

If you enable sync, Bird Hunter can store photo-backed records in your private iCloud account using Apple CloudKit. This is optional. Shadowfetch does not operate its own photo backend for Bird Hunter.

Photos And Sharing

If you add a photo, Bird Hunter saves a down-sampled thumbnail for fast list browsing and a higher-quality version for the full sighting record.

If you share a trophy card, Bird Hunter uses Apple’s system share sheet. The app does not silently post to Instagram, X, or any other network on your behalf.

Location

Bird Hunter can request location access if you choose to use current-location assist while logging a sighting. Exact coordinates are stored for your own records only and are not posted publicly through the app’s trophy-card flow.

Dataset Attribution

Bird Hunter uses BIRDBASE 2025.1 and AVONET v7 as bundled reference datasets. Their names remain visible in the app and support materials because their open licenses still require attribution to accompany the data.
