Fast PDF

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 9, 2026

Overview

Fast PDF is designed to work locally on the customer’s Apple device. The app does not require an account, does not operate a hosted document inbox, and does not upload document images or generated PDFs to a Shadowfetch-operated server.

Information Stored on Device

Fast PDF stores working data locally on the device, including the current rapid-session counters shown in the app and an optional Files folder bookmark if the customer approves a parent location for the dedicated Fast PDF folder workflow.

Camera Access

The app uses the device camera only to capture paper pages that the customer chooses to turn into PDFs. The images are processed locally for PDF creation.

Files and iCloud Drive

The customer decides where exported PDFs go. They can save through the Files picker one document at a time, or approve a location once so the app can create and reuse a Fast PDF folder. If the customer selects iCloud Drive, Apple handles syncing through the customer’s Apple account and Files infrastructure.

Analytics and Tracking

Fast PDF does not include analytics SDKs, ad SDKs, fingerprinting, or cross-app tracking.

Data Sharing

Shadowfetch does not sell, rent, or share document data from Fast PDF with third parties because the app does not collect that data on a Shadowfetch server in the first place.
